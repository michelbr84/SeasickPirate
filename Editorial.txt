The Pirate Who Was Afraid of the Sea  
Editorial and Production Plan  

---

**Author:** Marin Bloom  
**Target Audience:** Children aged 6–8 years  
**Genre:** Humorous picture book / early reader adventure  
**Theme:** Overcoming fear with humor; finding courage in small, brave actions  
**Moral:** Courage isn’t the absence of fear—it’s choosing to try, laugh, and keep going even when you’re scared.

### Production Logistics

- **File Formats:** High-resolution PDF for print; EPUB/MOBI for ebooks.  
- **Illustration Style:** Bright, engaging kid-friendly palette; clear character silhouettes; playful and rounded shapes (compatible with early reader audience).  
- **Printing Specs:** Standard 8” x 10” hardcover and paperback options; full-color throughout; durable paper suitable for children.  
- **Digital Formats:** Interactive read-aloud enabled eBook versions recommended.  

---

### Marketing & Outreach Channels

- Target audience: Parents, educators, librarians, and early readers.  
- Channels: Social media campaigns, children’s book fairs, library programs, school visits, influencer partnerships in parenting and education.  
- Materials: Preview excerpts, illustration teasers, guided breathing exercises inspired by Old Sal’s lesson, and sing-along video featuring Cookie Ned’s shanty.  

---

### Risk Management

- Ensuring tasteful, age-appropriate presentation of Pip’s seasickness to avoid anxiety triggers.  
- Flexible illustration schedule to incorporate editorial and sensitivity feedback.  
- Coordination of digital and print timelines to avoid market delays.
